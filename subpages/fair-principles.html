FAIR PRINCIPLES

Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable (FAIR) data are those that adhere to these four characteristics (FAIR). In a publication published in the journal Scientific Data in March 2016, a group of scientists and organizations defined the acronym and the guiding principles.

Because humans increasingly rely on computational support to deal with data due to the increase in volume, complexity, and creation speed of data, the FAIR principles place an emphasis on machine-actionability (i.e., the capacity of computational systems to find, access, interoperate, and reuse data with no or minimal human intervention).

When a dataset or database is marked with the acronym FAIR/O data, it means that it complies with the FAIR principles and also has an explicit data-capable open license.
